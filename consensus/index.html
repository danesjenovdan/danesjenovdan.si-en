The right to good life and solidarity

Everybody has the right to good health care, education, and housing. All should live in an encouraging and supportive society, be able to exercise the right to free time and leisure, have a happy childhood, creative youth, a fulfilled adulthood and safe elderliness. Everybody has the right to a future. The benchmark for success has too long been economic success, an individual's income inappropriately a condition for their happiness. Our measure is pleasure.

The right to the commons

The magnitude of our power is only visible when we join our individual powers. Thus, it is necessary to protect the commons and to manage it in a democratic and transparent way that aims for well-being of everybody. We do not endorse privatisation or destruction of natural resources and public spaces.

The right to proper political authority

Politics should not be perceived as a struggle for power. Instead, it should be used to protect common interests. We demand an open political space, withdrawal of existing power elites and strict focus on content rather than form or popularity. The civil sphere should be allowed to access and have direct influence over decision-making mechanisms.

The right to/of nature

Nature is the subject of protection, the interests of the natural environment our common interest, and the animals have the right to dignity. Progress, development and economic growth should not be an excuse for irresponsible behavior towards our natural environment.

The right to a human-friendly economy

Public debt is direct result of private enterprises, thus it should not have beeen made public and offloaded to the people. We want to create an environment in which socially and environmentally responsive companies will flourish. Everybody has the right to fulfilling work and a dignified life.

The right to inclusion

We reject divisions on basis of any personal circumstances. We should stop allowing the implementation of politics of exclusion that deny random social groups possibility to decent existence, push them to social margins, and appropriate their the power. The right to equality, which is guaranteed by the Constitution, should be practiced rather than just preached. The right to participation and human rights apply to everyone. That is not subject to discussion.
